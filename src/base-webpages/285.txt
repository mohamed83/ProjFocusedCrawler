Because you care: The Indian Express Citizens’ Initiative for Nepal earthquake
To help residents in the disaster zone pick up the pieces of what was once their lives, The Indian Express has set up a Relief Fund.











Comments

Email
Print

















By: Web Desk | New Delhi | 		
 Updated: April 26, 2015 8:10 pm 


 The April 25 earthquake in Nepal has killed over 2,200 men, women and children, devastated their towns and villages, destroyed roads, communication links. The number of dead is feared to go up as relief and rescue get under way. It may be months before the full magnitude of the tragedy becomes clear. On the Indian side, too, there have been deaths and damage. To help residents in the disaster zone pick up the pieces of what was once their lives, The Indian Express has set up a Relief Fund.
EXPRESS CITIZENS’ RELIEF FUND
Your contribution will be eligible for tax deduction under Section 80G.
Please mention your address, including PIN code, phone number and Permanent Account Number (PAN) so that we can send you your tax-exemption certificates. We will keep you informed on how your contribution is making a difference.
You may send your cheques payable to: Indian Express Citizens’ Relief Fund
