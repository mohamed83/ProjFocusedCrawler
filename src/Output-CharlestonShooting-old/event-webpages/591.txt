Hardee’s in Winston-Salem robbed at gunpoint; police looking for suspect


 10 Clemmonsville Road (Google Maps)


WINSTON-SALEM, N.C. — Police are investigating after a man allegedly robbed a Hardee’s restaurant at gunpoint Friday night in Winston-Salem.
Police said the suspect entered at 10 Clemmonsville Road shortly before 9:30 p.m. with a handgun and demanded the register and safe be opened.
The suspect took an undisclosed amount of cash and was last seen running toward a gold vehicle parked on the ramp to southbound US Highway 52, according to police.
No injuries were reported. The suspect is described as a black male, 6’1” and between 25-35 years old. Police said he was wearing blue jeans and a long sleeve black shirt.
The investigation is ongoing. Anyone with any information can call Winston-Salem police at 773-7700 or Crime Stoppers at 727-2800.
