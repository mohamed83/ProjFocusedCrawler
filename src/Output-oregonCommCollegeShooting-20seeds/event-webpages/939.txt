Students are given the opportunity to take what they learn in the classroom and apply it to the real world in a clinical setting. These unique opportunities ensure graduates are fully prepared to enter a fast-paced and evolving field. Nursing is also one of the

A strong nursing program will offer a foundation in the sciences, while preparing students for work in a clinical setting by providing hands-on experience. Graduates of four-year nursing programs are also qualified to take the National Council Licensing Examination to become a registered nurse.

The list comes from College Factual and is a ranking of colleges based on their overall quality.These schools offer programs that help prepare students for a successful and rewarding career in the medical field.


1. Columbia University: New York, NY


Columbia University is ranked as one of the 
 and top three in the state of New York. The nursing program offers students the opportunity to learn all aspects of the field, while preparing them for a successful career in medicine. The school offers a 
 (ETP) program that combines a BS and MS degree and teaches students the fundamentals of nursing in both a classroom and clinical setting before moving on to an advanced program. Whichever nursing path students decide to take at Columbia, graduates can be confident they are entering the field with the most comprehensive and professional training.


Graduates from Columbia enter the workforce earning an average early career salary of $69,000 and mid-career salary of $98,000.


2. New York University: New York, NY


Founded in 1831, New York University has a long history of offering academically challenging programs. The school employs a faculty that is committed to providing students with a strong education that prepares them for success in their chosen field. The College of Nursing gives students the knowledge and skills to provide excellent patient care. Nursing students are taught in a hands-on 
 to ensure they are well versed in the most up-to-date nursing practices. NYU offers both a 15-month accelerated program and traditional four-year program to give students the option to choose the track that works best for them. NYU graduates are well prepared to enter the profession, with almost all students passing the licensing exam for registered nurses on the first try and earning the highest average early career salary of $71,000.

3. Georgetown University: Washington, D.C.


Georgetown University has a legacy of offering a rigorous and world-class education that helps develop students to become leaders. The 
 is designed to offer a dynamic curriculum that introduces students to the field and equips them with the skills to offer top patient care. Georgetown blends classroom learning and clinical experience, so students are prepared to work in the healthcare field. Access to top facilities, including the Discovery Center and the O’Neill Family Foundation Clinical Simulation Center ensure students leave the program confident and ready for a successful career.


Whether they choose the four-year degree program or 16-month Accelerated Second Degree BSN, Georgetown students prove to find rewarding positions at the top medical facilities, earning an average early career salary of $64,000 and the highest average mid-career salary of $102,000.


4. Molloy College: Rockville Centre, NY


Molloy College is a private liberal arts school that is rooted in Catholic values. Its 
 and challenging academics ensure students work closely with faculty members to gain a comprehensive understanding of their chosen field. The 
 immerses students in the medical field to give them a well-rounded education. Students improve their technical and interpersonal skills in a variety of clinical settings before ending the program with a one-on-one practicum placement. Molloy nursing students are offered a variety of healthcare tracks and may chose to work toward an L.P.N. or R.N. degree. Dual degree programs also offer the opportunity gain specific certifications and advanced degrees.


Whichever direction students choose to go, graduates have found successful careers earning average early career and mid-career salaries of $70,000 and $91,000, respectively.

5. University of Pennsylvania, Philadelphia, PA


The University of Pennsylvania is an Ivy League research school that is notorious for offering a demanding curriculum in numerous fields. Founded in 1740, Penn has a tradition of developing creativity, entrepreneurship and innovation. The undergraduate nursing program uses a liberal arts curriculum to integrate sciences courses with arts and humanities to provide students with a fundamental knowledge of healthcare. Along with offering a BS in Nursing, students at Penn may pursue dual degree programs or enter the 
 that culminates in a Ph.D. Graduates of the Penn nursing program find positions in a variety of healthcare centers, making $63,000 at the entry level and $87,000 by mid-career.

6. University of California-Los Angeles


UCLA has a mission of driving students towards accomplishing their goals through optimism and perseverance. The School of Nursing works to help prepare students for a career in an ever-changing field. The curriculum is designed to expose students to the healthcare system and help teach them how to lead the transformation and development of the field. Students in the 
 graduate with the skills to handle everything from population to individual-level care. They are also eligible to take the National Council Licensing Examination to become a registered nurse. UCLA students are successful in the nursing field, earning an average early career salary of $65,000 and mid-career salary of $86,000.

7. Johns Hopkins University: Baltimore, MD


Johns Hopkins University has a strong reputation for being an excellent medical university. The school employs a faculty that is world-renowned for their success in the field. The School of Nursing offers a 
 that helps lead students towards a career in nursing. Students are immersed in the field and train rigorously to build a strong foundation for their career. Upon completion, students may take the National Council Licensure Examination. Some decide to pursue one of the many specialized nursing master’s offered at the university. A degree from Johns Hopkins is a fundamental step towards a nursing career. Graduates enter the field earning an average early career salary of $56,000, increasing to $73,000 at the mid-career level.


8. Simmons College: Boston, MA


Simmons College is a private women’s school that provides students with a strong liberal arts education. The School of Nursing and Health Sciences works to prepare students for a rewarding career in nursing by offering a combination of clinical and research opportunities. Students learn from professors who still practice in the field and become mentors as students move through the 
. Nursing majors graduate with an advanced knowledge of the nursing field along with the skills to provide quality patient care. Simmons students apply their training to positions in hospitals, clinics and other healthcare centers, earning en average mid-career salary of $79,000.

9. Drexel University: Philadelphia, PA


Drexel University is a research institution that teaches students how to apply findings to real-world situations. The multidisciplinary curriculum focuses on providing students with the knowledge and skills to excel in their chosen field. The nursing program at Drexel lays the foundation for a successful medical career. Nursing students have the option to participate in a 
 that incorporates three, six-month periods of full employment into the curriculum. While at their fulltime positions, students gain real-world, hand-on experience that supplement classroom learning. Students receive top compensation after graduation, with average early career earnings of  $63,000 and mid-career salary of $76,000.


10. Quinnipiac University: Hamden, Conn.


Quinnipiac University gives students a high quality education in a 
. Small class sizes afford students the opportunity to work closely with faculty members. Students are able to collaborate with professors through research opportunities and expand their knowledge with hands-on experiences. Nursing majors are equipped with the skills to provide top medical care on a personalized basis. The traditional and accelerated 
 prepare students to enter the workforce or pursue graduate study. Those who find jobs after graduation typically earn an average early career salary of $67,000 and mid-career salary of $76,000.
