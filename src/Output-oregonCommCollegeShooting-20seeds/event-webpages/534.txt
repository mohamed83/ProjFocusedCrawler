On July 15, Ryan Patrick Jones, a 22-year-old student at the University of Alabama, was arrested for 
 possessing over 10,000 pills of the anti-anxiety medication, Xanax, and 141 grams of marijuana, an estimated street value of $75,000.


Sgt. Brent Blankley, a Tuscaloosa police spokesperson, told 
 that Jones’ arrest came after a month long investigation into the alleged drug dealer.


Jones has been charged with possession of a controlled substance, first-degree possession of marijuana and one count of selling a controlled substance within three miles of a school.


RELATED: 


This is not the first time that University of Alabama students have been caught in large drug busts. 
 in 2014, 64 students were arrested during a West Alabama Task Force Roundup for selling and possessing various narcotics.


According to the university’s 
, the school “will take disciplinary action against a student, group of students, or student organization for unauthorized use, possession, sale, or distribution of any controlled substance or illegal drug that occurs on university property or as part of any university activity.”


Chris Bryant, a spokesperson for the University of Alabama, said in an emailed statement to USA Today College that the school is aware of the situation. According to Bryant, Jones has been referred to the Office of Student Conduct and has been placed on 
.


He is currently being held at the Tuscaloosa County Jail on a $35,000 bond.


Alex Samuels is a University of Texas-Austin student and a breaking news correspondent for USA TODAY College
