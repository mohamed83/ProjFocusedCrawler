There are many advantages to staying in-state when it comes to getting your education. Not only will you save money on transportation to and from home, many public colleges offer generous discounts to students in their own state. There are many excellent colleges located all throughout the U.S., and chances are good you can find a reasonable priced school near you that has the programs and services important to you.


This ranking is based on College Factual’s Best Colleges for the Money Ranking and reveals some of the 
 to in-state students who receive financial aid. Are any of these schools on your radar? You may want to take a look at what they offer, even if you have never considered them before.


*Note that prices are estimates based on the colleges reported data from prior years. Each student’s unique circumstance will dictate the amount of financial aid they can receive.


1. University of Virginia-Main Campus: Charlottesville, Va.


University of Virginia campus  is seen on December 6, 2014 in Charlottesville, Virginia. (Photo by Jay Paul/Getty Images)


The University of Virginia is a large public university ranked 
. The school is fairly selective, only accepting about 30% of the students that apply. The excellent tuition discount makes the university a great buy for in-state students. Those students who are expecting financial aid pay an average of $10,700 per year. Most students graduate in 4.1 years so that brings the total cost of the degree to about $43,900.


2. University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill: Chapel Hill, N.C.


Chapel Hill is ranked 
 in North Carolina with most students graduating in 4.2 years. The average yearly cost for in-state students who can expect to receive aid is $11,400. The average total cost of the degree for those students would be $47,900.


3. University of Delaware: Newark, Del.


The University Delaware is considered the top school in Delaware as well as being ranked in the top 100 schools in the country. Students typically graduate in 4.2. In-State students who receive aid usually pay an average of $11,400 a year, with the total cost of their degree being about $47,900.


4. College of William and Mary: Williamsburg, Va.


Coming in fourth on this list is another Virginia school, The College of William and Mary, which ranks 4

5. University of Michigan-Ann Arbor: Ann Arbor, Mich.


The University of Michigan is the top ranked school in the state. It is a 
 with a low student to faculty ratio and a low student loan default rate. Students take about 4.2 years to graduate and average about $13,100 per year. The total cost of a degree from Michigan is $54,900.


6. University of Florida: Gainesville, Fla.


The University of Florida is a large and diverse public university top ranked in the state. Students take about 4.3 years to graduate. Costs are affordable for in-state students, with those receiving aid averaging about $14,100 a year. The total cost of a degree from the University of Florida would be about $60,300.


7. North Carolina State University at Raleigh: Raleigh, N.C.


The 2
