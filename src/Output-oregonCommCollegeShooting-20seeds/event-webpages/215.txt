Hannah Tyburski, daughter of Janet Tyburski, is being charged with four felony counts in connection with her sister’s death (Photo courtesy of Lorain County Sheriff’s Office)


An Oberlin College student has been charged with four felony counts after co-conspiring to cover up a homicide.


Hannah Tyburski, 21, was apprehended June 30 by North Ridgeville police and processed at Lorain County Jail, according to the 
.


The Lakewood, Ohio native was charged with obstructing justice, tampering with evidence and abusing a corpse, according to the 
.


RELATED: 


Authorities told 
 that Hannah Tyburski’s mother, Janet, suffocated Hannah’s 26-year-old sister, Rachele Tyburski, to death. The two later moved the body from their home and dumped it in a North Ridgeville field on March 15. Police discovered Rachele Tyburski’s body later that day.


According to 
, police released photos of Rachele Tyburski’s tattoos to facilitate the identification of her body. Upon seeing the photos, Janet Tyburski called in to confirm that Rachele was her daughter. Janet Tyburski was charged with murder and arrested May 5, 2015. She is being held on a $2 million bond.


 reported that Hannah Tyburski was released June 30 on a $7,000 bail.


At this point in time, the women’s motives are unclear. Their trial date, according to the 
,
 is pending.
