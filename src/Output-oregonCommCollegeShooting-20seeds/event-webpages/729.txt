Harvard is a well-known and well-ranked university with a low student loan default rate. Students take an average of 4.1 years to graduate. If you get aid, you’ll pay around $20,700 a year with the total cost of the degree being $85,500.


5. Brigham Young University-Provo: Provo, Utah


Brigham Young appears in our top ten best for the money schools for both in-state and out-of-state students. This unique college offers generous aid to all students, with the average yearly cost being $11,000 per year and $54,400 for the degree. Students graduate in an average of 4.9 years. 6. Sewanee-The University of the South: Sewanee, Tenn.


Sewanee is a highly selective university with an on-time graduation rate of 74%. The average length of time students take to graduate is 4.1 years. Students who receive aid will pay an average of $19,800 per year or $81,200 for the full cost of their degree.


7. Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art: New York


Cooper Union was long known for being a tuition free college. In recent years they have had to charge tuition, but still keep costs reasonable for students, with those receiving aid averaging $16,400 a year. Students typically graduate in 4.3 years, making the average total cost of a degree at Cooper Union $70,000.
