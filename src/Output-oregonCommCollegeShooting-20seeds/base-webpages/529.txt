ROSEBURG, Ore. -- Wildlife officers are working to remove a cougar spotted near Umpqua Community College.




The Douglas County Sheriff's Office said the animal was seen numerous times near the campus and surrounding neighborhoods since Monday.




The cougar is considered a public threat.


















If you see the animal, call 9-1-1.




ODFW recommends that people take precautions to avoid conflicts with cougars:




* Be aware of your surroundings at all times




* Keep children close and teach them about wildlife




* Don’t leave food and garbage outside




* Feed pets indoors and keep them inside at night




* Remove heavy brush from near the house and play areas




* Install motion-activated outdoor lights




* Keep areas around bird feeders clean
