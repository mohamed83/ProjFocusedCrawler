PORTLAND, Ore. -- Portland Police officers will deploy an extra presence at area schools in light of the mass shooting at Umpqua Community College.


 and several others were injured Thursday morning when a gunman opened fire in a classroom. 


Portland Police Bureau Chief Lawrence P. O'Dea requested officers provide extra presence at Portland schools to enhance public safety. There is currently no threat to Portland schools.


Additionally, PPB has offered resources to the Douglas County Sheriff's Office and Oregon State Police.


Portland Mayor Charlie Hales said 12 people from the city's trauma intervention team, three public information officers and two chief officers are heading to Roseburg.
