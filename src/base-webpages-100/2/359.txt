BABAY | Hagupit continues to move out of PH; no more storm signals

						By: Jose Bimbo F. Santos, InterAksyon.com
						December 9, 2014 11:53 AM					
 






			Hagupit track as of 11 a.m., 9 December 2014. DOST/PAGASA
		
 



 







Tweet




 

InterAksyon.comThe online news portal of TV5
MANILA - (Update 6:10pm) Tropical Depression 'Hagupit' (local name Ruby) continues to move away from the country in line with earlier projections it would exit the Philippine area of responsibility either by Wednesday evening or Thursday early morning. all storm signals were earlier lifted Tuesday morning.According to the 5 pm weather forecast by PAGASA, Hagupit was last seen 215 km west-northwest of Calapan City, Oriental Mindoro with winds of 45 kph moving at a speed of 13 kph.PAGASA said light to moderate rainshowers and thunderstorms can be expected in Metro Manila, Central Luzon, Calabarzon, Bicol region, and Mimaropa. Cagayan, Cordillera, and Ilocos will experience cloudy skies with light rains, while the rest of the country is expected to have cloudy skies with isolated rainshowers or thunderstorms.Moderate to strong winds blowing from the northeast to east is expected to prevail over Northern and Central Luzon.Earlier Tuesday, PAGASA terminated all heavy rainfall warning alerts across the country, as Ruby further weakened to 45 kilometers per hour near the center and was headed toward the West Philippine Sea.
As of 10 a.m. Tuesday, 9 December 2014, the center of Ruby “was estimated based on all available data at 135 km West Northwest of Calapan City, Oriental Mindoro (13.8°N, 120.0°E).”
Ruby moves at the speed of 13 kph and is expected to leave the country by Thursday morning.
Estimated rainfall amount is from 5 to 15 mm per hour (or moderate to heavy) within the 200 km diameter of Ruby.
Together with the northeast monsoon, Ruby will cause rough to very rough sea conditions over the seaboards of Luzon.
The weather bureau advised fisherfolk and those using small sea craft are advised not to venture out over these seaboards. 
Forecast positions • 24 hour (tomorrow morning): 450 km west of Calapan City, Oriental Mindoro • 48 hour (Thursday morning): 765 km west of Calapan City, Oriental Mindoro or outside the Philippine Area of Responsibility (PAR) • 72 hour (Friday morning): 1,010 km west southwest of Calapan City, Oriental Mindoro
The next weather bulletin is at 11 p.m. today, the Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical, and Astronomical Service Administration said.
