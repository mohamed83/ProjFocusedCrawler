Typhoon “Ruby” makes landfall in Eastern Samar, storm signal declared in 49 areas

 Tanya Aurora
 December 7, 2014










Typhoon “Ruby” (International Name: Hagupit) made its landfall in Dolores, Eastern Samar at 9:15 p.m. on Saturday, according to the Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services (PAGASA) weather forecaster Jori Loiz.
The US National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) caught on infrared satellite how the typhoon made its landfall.

After its first landfall, Typhoon “Ruby” is expected to make five more landfalls near the areas of Romblon and Mindoro.
Public Storm Warning Signals have been hoisted in the following areas:

PAGASA warned residents in the said areas should prepare for possible flooding and landslides. Adding that Typhoon “Ruby” and the northeast monsoon will bring rough to very rough sea conditions over the seaboards of northern Luzon and Visayas, eastern seaboard of central and southern Luzon, and northern and eastern seaboards of Mindanao.
Fisherfolk and those using small seacraft are advised not to venture out over these seaboards.
