Typhoon “Ruby” (International Name: Hagupit) Enters Philippine Area of Responsibility (PAR)

 Phil Trends Bloggers
 December 4, 2014










Typhoon with International name “Hagupit” has entered the Philippine Area of Responsibility (PAR) on Thursday, December 4, 2014 at 3 a.m.

The typhoon was named by the Philippine Atmospheric Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration (PAGASA) weather bureau “Ruby” as it entered the country.
According to PAGASA, the typhoon was last spotted at 942 km East Northeast of Hinatuan, Surigao del Sur or 1,023 East of Surigao City  (9.4°N, 134.8°E).
Typhoon Ruby has maximum sustained winds of 175 kilometers per hour (kph) near the center and gustiness of up to 210 kph. It is forecast to move West Northwest at 25 kph with 75% chance of hitting the land and 25% of it changing track.







According to PAGASA weather forecaster Samuel Duran, if typhoon “Ruby” maintains its current speed and direction, the effects will be felt in the country on Friday.
The National Disaster Risk Reduction Management Council (NDRRMC) and PAGASA declared on Wednesday night over 44 provinces as critical areas and are expected to experience heavy to intense rains and strong winds ranging from 95 to 110 kph.
Listed below are the areas where class suspensions have been declared due to typhoon “Ruby” (International name: Hagupit).
Areas with suspended classes in all levels:

Biliran Province (December 4 to 5)*
Tacloban City (December 4 to 5, 2014)
Catbalogan, Samar (Decemer 4 to 5, 2014)

*Culaba, Biliran suspension is on December 5, 2014 only
schools, colleges, and universities that declared suspension of classes:

University of the Philippines Visayas (Tacloban) (Classes to resume December 11)
Visayas State University (December 4 to 5)
