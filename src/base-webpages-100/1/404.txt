The Philippines: Police Arrest Suspects in an Online Blackmailing Syndicate

By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSAUG. 22, 2014
 
Inside








Continue reading the main story

Continue reading the main story





Continue reading the main story
Share This Page

Continue reading the main story


Philippine police have arrested eight people suspected of being members of an online syndicate accused of blackmailing more than 1,000 Hong Kong and Singapore residents after luring them into exposing themselves in front of a webcam, an official said Friday. The raid on the syndicate’s locations in northern Bulacan Province on Thursday was a follow-up to an operation backed by Interpol in April in which 58 Filipinos were arrested, and 100 were linked to the extortion in a subsequent investigation in May. The victims were from all over the world, including a Scottish teenager who committed suicide after being blackmailed. Victims would be lured into cybersex on Skype and recorded, and a video link would be sent to their Facebook accounts unless they paid at least $3,000, said the leader of the police anti-cybercrime group, Gilbert Sosa.
