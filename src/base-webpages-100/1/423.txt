Strong winds fanned the flames racing through a row of shanties nearly half a mile long in a village in Quezon City, a suburb of Manila, before dawn on Thursday, said a local official, Noel Carino. Firefighters struggled to get near the burning homes because of narrow alleys. A Bureau of Fire Protection spokesman, Renato Marcial, said that nearly a thousand houses, mostly shanties and huts, had been destroyed, displacing about 4,000 families.
