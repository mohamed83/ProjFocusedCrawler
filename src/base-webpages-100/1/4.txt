6 Q’s About the News | Typhoon Hagupit Hits the Philippines


				By Shannon Doyne 
December 8, 2014 4:31 am
December 8, 2014 4:31 am



PhotoResidents in Legazpi, the Philippines, protect themselves from the rain and heavy winds of Typhoon Hagupit on Sunday. Related ArticleCredit Aaron Favila/Associated Press




6 Q’s About the News
Read the article and answer the news questions below.

See All 6 Q’s About the News »



In “As Typhoon Hagupit Hits Philippines, Evacuees Express Relief” Austin Ramzy reports on .
WHEN did Typhoon Hagupit churn across the Philippines?
HOW many people were forced to evacuate their homes?
WHY did Eleanor Llaneta say she was willing to evacuate, something she may not have considered in the past?
HOW does this typhoon compare to Typhoon Haiyan?
WHERE is the storm expected to reach before it dissipates?

WHO is Kate Marshall?
WHAT makes Albay Province one of the most vulnerable areas in the Philippines when it comes to typhoons?
HOW has disaster preparedness improved there in recent years?
WHY do officials say that the area is “not out of the woods yet”?

For Higher-Order Thinking
WHAT do you think it’s like to live in a place that experiences extreme weather events?
HOW would you react if you had to evacuate your home somewhat often due to danger caused by typhoons?
