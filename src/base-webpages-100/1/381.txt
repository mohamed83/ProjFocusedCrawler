The deal grants largely Muslim areas of the southern Mindanao region greater political autonomy in exchange for an end to armed rebellion, although other insurgent groups have vowed to keep fighting for full independence. The deal calls for rebel arms to be put “beyond use” by 2016, a government negotiator said. More than 120,000 people have died in separatist violence since the 1970s in Mindanao.
