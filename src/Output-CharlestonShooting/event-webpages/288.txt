Suspect wanted for allegedly shooting teenager in the Bronx




Posted 11:14 PM, August 22, 2015, by 
Alyssa Zauderer
 




Facebook
Twitter
Reddit
Pinterest
Email
Google
 






Joe Furman, 30, is wanted in connection with a shooting in the Bronx that injured a teenager. (DCPI)


CLAREMONT, The Bronx — Police are searching for a man they say shot a teenage girl in the arm Thursday evening in the Bronx.


Police say the shooting happened just after 9 p.m., in front of 1333 Webster Ave.


The suspect who police identified as Joe Furman, 30, of the Bronx allegedly shot a 14-year-old girl in the arm before fleeing the scene.


The victim was taken to Lincoln Hospital where she was treated and released.


Furman is described as being 6-foot, weighing 185 pounds and has tattoos on his arms and torso. Police say he also goes by “JFK Joe Fonze.”
