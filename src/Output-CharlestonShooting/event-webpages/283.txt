Knife-wielding man shot by police in New Jersey: cops




Posted 12:33 PM, June 20, 2015, by 
Myles Miller
, 
Updated at 07:24pm, June 20, 2015
 




Facebook
Twitter
Reddit
Pinterest
Email
Google
 












ORANGE, N.J. — A man is in critical condition after a confrontation with police at a New Jersey Dunkin’ Donuts.


The incident began at approximately 9 a.m., when East Orange police responded to reports of a man wielding a knife at a Dunkin’ Donuts on Central Avenue, said Anthony Ambrose, chief of detectives for the Essex County Prosecutors Office.


Authorities responded to the area after getting a report of a man with a knife. They followed the man but when he would not drop the knife, they attempted to subdue him with pepper spray.


Police say the suspect then lunged at an officer, stabbing her in the shoulder as she fired her gun. The supervisor on duty also fired off a shot, authorities say.  The officers fired a total of six shots, but it is not known how many times the man was struck.


An officer with Orange police use pepper spray to try to contain the suspect, that’s when he allegedly struck a female officer in the shoulder with the knife.


The injured officer along with a sergeant began firing at the suspect. The officers fired a total of six shots it’s unclear how many times The suspect was struck.


The officers were treated at an area hospital and released.


The suspect is listed in critical condition at University Hospital in Newark.
