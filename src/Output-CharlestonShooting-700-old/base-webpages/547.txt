        TWEETLINKEDINCOMMENTEMAILMORECharleston, SC (AP, WLTX) - A South Carolina legislative leader says the church's pastor, State Sen. Clementa Pinckney, is among those killed in a shooting at a historic black church in downtown Charleston that authorities are calling a hate crime.House Minority Leader Todd Rutherford says Pinckney, 41, was killed along with eight others in the Wednesday night shooting at Emanuel AME Church. Law enforcement and the Charleston County Coroner's office have not confirmed the identity of any of the nine victims.Pinckney had led the church since 2010.The Democrat was first elected to the South Carolina House of Representatives in 1997, and in 2001, and voters sent him to the Senate in 2001. He represents an area that includes Allendale, Beaufort, Charleston, Colleton, Hampton and Jasper Counties.According to his biography on the General Assembly's website, Pinckney also served on the board of directors of Southern Mutual Insurance Company. He was married with two children.Pinckney held degrees from Allen University and the University of South Carolina.Read or Share this story: http://www.wcnc.com/story/news/2015/06/18/pastor-state-senator-among-the-dead-in-mass-shooting/28910953/
