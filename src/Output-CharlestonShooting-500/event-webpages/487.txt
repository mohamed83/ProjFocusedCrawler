2 people injured in shooting at Greensboro restaurant identified

Posted 5:59 am, May 22, 2015, by Web Staff, Updated at 03:54pm, May 22, 2015 

FacebookTwitterGooglePinterestRedditEmail 



Photo Gallery


 View Gallery (4 images)






 


GREENSBORO, N.C. — Two people were taken to the hospital Friday morning after a shooting at Jake’s Diner.
The shooting happened around 4 a.m. at 2206 S. Holden Road.
Officers discovered the first victim was taken to Moses Cone Hospital in a private vehicle. He was identified as 24-year-old Dexter Lavoy Pittman.
The second victim was taken to Moses Cone Hospital by EMS. He was identified as 27-year-old Phillip Rayshawn Wilkerson.
Both are listed in stable condition.
The investigation is ongoing.
Anyone with information is asked to call Crime Stoppers at (336) 373-1000.
