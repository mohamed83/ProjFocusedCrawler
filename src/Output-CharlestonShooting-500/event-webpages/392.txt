Local Churches Holding Prayer Vigils Friday in Wake of S.C. Shooting


 


DES MOINES, Iowa — On Friday, three local churches will host prayer vigils and services to remember the lives lost in the Charleston, South Carolina church shooting.
On Wednesday, 21-year-old, Dylann Roof, shot and killed nine church members including the pastor of the Emanuel African Methodist Episcopal Church during an evening bible study.
Roof admitted to the killings and is charged with nine counts of murder and one count of possession of a firearm during the commission of a violent crime.
Members of the Des Moines faith community will host events throughout the day.
A prayer vigil will take place at St. Paul AME Church from noon to 1p.m. The church is located at 201 Day Street in Des Moines.
The Temple B’nai Jeshurun will host an interfaith Shabbat service from 6p.m – 7p.m. Reverend Dr. Brigitte Black from Bethel AME in Des Moines will be the featured speaker. The event is located at 5101 Grand Avenue in Des Moines.
This evening, Bethel AME Church invites the community to its worship service at 7:30 p.m. The church is located at 1528 E. University in Des Moines.
All the events are free and open to the public.
